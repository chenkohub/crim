📋 Recommended Study Flow

A daily routine built on cognitive science research. Each step targets a different learning principle for maximum retention.

Daily Study Session (~65 min)
1
Pre-Test: Prime Your Brain (5 min)
Take a diagnostic quiz on today's topic BEFORE reviewing. Getting answers wrong primes deeper encoding through the forward testing effect.
2
Review: Lecture Notes & Why Guides (20 min)
Read the condensed notes, then explore policy rationale. Understanding "why" activates elaborative interrogation — making rules 2-3x more memorable.
3
Recall: SRS Flashcards & Rule Drills (15 min)
Daily SRS review (cards due today), then write key rules from memory. Active recall produces 50-80% retention vs 10-15% for passive reading.
4
Apply: Issue Spotters & Flowcharts (15 min)
Work mixed-topic fact patterns. Interleaving produces 25-76% better transfer than blocked practice. Use flowcharts to verify your analysis.
5
Teach: Feynman Prompts (10 min)
Explain one concept in plain language. The Feynman Technique exposes the illusion of competence — if you can't explain it simply, you don't know it yet.
6
Weekly: Full Practice Exam (3 hours)
Once a week, do a full timed exam under realistic conditions. Compare your answers against model answers and self-grade with the rubric.
